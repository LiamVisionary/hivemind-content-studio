# Monetization operating model

The studio supports three revenue loops without hard-coding unverified earnings claims:

1. **Content retainers:** produce approved clips, faceless shorts, and animated explainers for clients on a recurring package.
2. **Owned-channel funnels:** publish original content that drives tracked affiliate, newsletter, product, or app conversions.
3. **Performance clipping campaigns:** use only campaign-approved source material and match rendered clips to tracked opportunities.

Auto Clipper's monetization tables retain opportunity path, content/rights requirements, target platforms, payout-model notes, and fit scoring. The content manifest adds the production and publish receipt required to connect output to results.

## Initial offers

| Offer | Deliverable | Proof metric |
|---|---|---|
| Pilot batch | 10 approved shorts from owned/client media | accepted outputs and turnaround |
| Monthly content engine | 30-60 multi-platform assets | retention, saves, clicks, qualified leads |
| Animation/explainer add-on | scripted scene plan, generated assets, final video | approved deliverable and conversion use |
| Distribution add-on | QA, scheduling, receipts, weekly report | successful publishes and attributable outcomes |

## Closed loop

Track platform, content lane, niche, hook/format, publish receipt, retention, completion, clicks, conversions, and revenue per run. Remix winners; stop formats that repeatedly fail retention and conversion thresholds. Platform monetization and affiliate terms change, so verify current eligibility and payout rules before using them in forecasts.
