Create a 15-second hyper-realistic UGC product ad for [PRODUCT]. Casual realistic UGC vlog, hyper-realistic iPhone aesthetic, no CGI look, candid relatable. Attach a character reference image (see the UGC character reference template) and keep it locked in every scene.

FORMAT: 9:16 | ~15s | handheld phone-camera selfie throughout, slight micro-shake, quick vlog cuts, no artificial dolly, no stabilizer-perfect motion, no cinematic zoom.

GLOBAL LOCKS
- CHARACTER LOCK: [CHARACTER — age, build, hair, energy], identical to the attached reference in every scene. Restate the reference's named skin imperfections; video models smooth them out otherwise. Same exact look in every cut.
- OUTFIT LOCK: same casual outfit as the reference, every garment identical across scenes.
- ENVIRONMENT LOCK: [SCENE — lived-in details, light direction, time of day].
- PRODUCT LOCK: every visible element of [PRODUCT] preserved from the product reference. No warped labels, no distorted packaging, no fake branding. If the product has any visible text, generate a clean product reference image first.
- VOICE LOCK: soft warm voice, calm casual vlogger tone, natural accent, conversational and intimate like talking to a friend on FaceTime, unhurried, never salesy. Reuse the exact same voice description in every production — the wording is the voice fingerprint.
- TEXT: none. No on-screen text, no captions, no logos (added in post).

SHOT SEQUENCE (alternate on-camera and voiceover; every on-camera beat needs all three anchors: phone held at chest height in selfie position; direct steady eye contact with the lens; visible natural mouth movement lip-synced to the line)
- [00:00-00:02 | Hook] on-camera: "[HOOK LINE]"
- [00:02-00:04 | Product reveal] macro shot of [PRODUCT]. Voiceover: what it is, casual.
- [00:04-00:06 | Setup] close-up of hands doing the first product action. On-camera: light explanation.
- [00:06-00:08 | Demo / hero] macro of the product doing its core function with realistic weighted physics — this is the beat that sells.
- [00:08-00:10 | Proof] the result on screen, face enters frame reacting. On-camera: genuine reaction.
- [00:10-00:12 | Second use] quick second angle. Voiceover: versatility line.
- [00:12-00:14 | Beauty cuts] 3 rapid close-up cuts of finishing details. On-camera: payoff line.
- [00:14-00:15 | Soft CTA] leaning casually, product visible. On-camera: "[CTA]"

LIP SYNC: when the face is in frame, full natural visible mouth articulation lip-synced to dialogue. Voice continuity seamless across all cuts.

START FRAME: every character who appears or speaks anywhere in the clip must be visible in the start frame.

PHYSICS: realistic weighted physics for every interaction — nothing floats, nothing clips; liquids keep surface tension, fabric drapes realistically, hair has subtle flyaways.

STYLE NEGATIVES: no CGI sheen, no plastic skin, no airbrushing, no beauty filter, no warped hands, no extra fingers, no stiff movement, no robotic face, no bad lip sync, no jitter, no flicker, no glossy commercial lighting, no studio lighting, no commercial color grading, no slow motion, no posed model energy, no on-screen text, no captions, no logos.

Script rule: no em dashes anywhere in spoken lines or captions. Use a period and start a new sentence.
