Create a short UGC-style ad for [PRODUCT] in the accidental-discovery format (the shape of "been shopping at Lidl for 8 years and NOW I find this???").

Beats:
1. [Hook] on-camera genuine-surprise energy naming the specific pain with real specificity: "[SPECIFIC PAIN]" — "8 years at Lidl", not "grocery shopping".
2. [Discovery] the product found mid-task, not reviewed: [DISCOVERY MOMENT]. The creator was doing the task anyway and stumbled into it.
3. [Reaction] disbelief that this existed the whole time; the feeling of being late is what the viewer catches.

Rules:
- Specificity is the mechanism. Vague pain kills the format; name the store, the years, the exact annoyance.
- The product must appear as found, never presented — no unboxing energy, no feature tour.
- Realism system applies: locked character reference, lived-in setting, phone-camera language, full negative stack.
